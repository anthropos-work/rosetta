| **D-M219-9** | **The coverage sweep and the Playthrough runner both grew a REMOTE-HOST seam (`COVERAGE_HOST` / `PT_HOST` + scheme), rather than the demo being moved local to suit the harness.** | The asserts went unrun for four releases partly because **the harness could not be pointed at the demo**: both runners hardcoded `localhost`, and the demo lives on a tailnet VM. The sweep must run from **the vantage a presenter actually has**. `--reset-only` splits the DB half (host-only) from the browser half (peer-only). | 2026-07-14 |
| **D-M219-10** | **R-5's fix is a FENCE over every shipped preset role, not a patch of the two broken ones.** `TestShippedPresets_EveryHeroRoleClassifies` reads the REAL presets and fails on any hero role that classifies to `curatedNone`. | Patching "Data Analyst" + "Operations Specialist" would fix today's demo and leave the **next** org to rediscover the junk tail on a presenter's screen — which is precisely how this one survived: M42e curated `software`+`sales` (the only orgs then), M51 added a third org with roles in neither family, and the classifier's comment blessed the fall-through as *"no regression for an unclassified role"*. A silent fall-through to the flat pool **is** the regression. The fence makes the next unclassified role fail at `go test`, not four releases later. | 2026-07-14 |
| **D-M219-11** | **Ben's role CHANGED in the shipped preset ("Operations Specialist" → "Operations Analyst"), rather than inventing an "Operations Specialist" job_role.** | The role simply **does not exist** in the public taxonomy (the preset comment claiming it was a "resolving public role" was false), and the seeding contract's first rule is **never fabricate a taxonomy node** — a non-resolving name must drop, not be invented. `Operations Analyst` resolves. It carries no `job_role_skills` of its own, which is exactly what the new curated `operations` pool is for. Applied to **both** the demo preset and `pt-world`. | 2026-07-14 |
| **D-M219-12** | **The 5-cycle cold battery runs at FINAL code — after F-13 and R-5 — not before.** | M218 **D13**: a demo-runtime change restarts the 5-cycle count. Both F-13 (`ant-academy.sh`, on the bring-up path) and R-5 (the seeder's curated pools, on the seed path) are demo-runtime changes. Running the battery before them would grade a binary the demo no longer ships — the exact "status artifact that outlives the thing it describes" pattern (D17). The rext **pin guard** enforced this independently: the bring-up REFUSED to run a clone whose HEAD did not match `.agentspace/rext.tag`. Tagged `cue-to-cue-m219-r5` and re-pinned. | 2026-07-14 |
| **D-M219-13** | **The junk-tail fix is a FALLBACK-LADDER change (`role → curated → flat`), not a wider allow-list.** | R-5's curated `operations` pool was **fully populated and unused**: `skillsForRole` fell back **role → FLAT**, and `combinedNamedPool` draws its *role* tier from that function — so for a role with zero `job_role_skills` (`Operations Analyst`) tier 1 **was already the junk head** and filled the quota before the curated tier was reached. No allow-list, however long, could have fixed that; the pool was never read. Making `flat` genuinely **last** is the only fix that makes the curated tier mean anything for such a role. Both twins (`namedSkillRefs` + `taxonomyRefs`) carried it — which is why even the hero's **verified** chain certified him in `15Five`. | 2026-07-14 |

This is the concrete justification for the battery being a DoD item rather than a formality. | 2026-07-14 |
