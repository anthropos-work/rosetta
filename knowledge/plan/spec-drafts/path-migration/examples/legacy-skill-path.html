Giving Difficult Feedback to a Direct Report

Intermediate ⏱ 3h 20m total 📚 4 chapters · 14 steps 🎯 1 graded simulation 🏢 Enterprise · in-app

Feedback is the single highest-leverage thing a manager does — and the thing most managers avoid. This path turns vague, anxiety-driven "we need to talk" moments into clear, kind, and effective conversations. You'll learn a repeatable structure (Situation–Behavior–Impact), the Radical Candor model for caring personally while challenging directly, why the classic "feedback sandwich" backfires, and how to handle the reactions that actually happen — defensiveness, silence, tears, and pushback. You finish in a graded roleplay where you deliver real feedback to a simulated report and get scored on empathy, clarity, and outcome.

Chapter 2 · The Frameworks  ›  Step 1 of 4

SBI explained: Situation → Behavior → Impact

▶
▶ Bunny Stream · narrated by Marcus Reeve 04:12 / 18:42

SBI is the spine of every good piece of feedback because it strips out the one thing that makes people defensive: judgment. Instead of "you're careless" (a character verdict), you describe a Situation, the specific Behavior you observed, and its concrete Impact. The person can argue with a verdict; they can't argue with what they actually did.

The three parts

  • Situation — anchor it in time and place so it's a real event, not a pattern accusation. "In yesterday's client call at 2pm…"
  • Behavior — observable facts only. What a camera would have caught. "…you interrupted Priya twice before she finished her point."
  • Impact — the effect on you, the work, or the team. "…and afterwards she went quiet and didn't share the migration risk she'd flagged to me earlier."

What it sounds like in the room

Manager: "I want to talk about the client call yesterday afternoon. (Situation) Twice, you cut in before Priya finished her point. (Behavior) I noticed she stopped contributing after that, and she'd had a migration risk to raise that never made it into the room. (Impact) How did you see it?"
Report: "Honestly I didn't realize I cut her off — I was just trying to keep us on time."
Manager: "That makes sense, and timekeeping matters. Let's find a way to do both — can we try a 'parking lot' for tangents so people still get to finish?"
✓ Do
  • Lead with one specific event, not "lately…"
  • Use observable behavior — what a camera saw
  • Name the impact, then ask for their view
  • Hold the silence after you speak
✕ Don't
  • Open with "you always" / "you never"
  • Mind-read intent ("you clearly don't care")
  • Bury it in a sandwich of fake praise
  • Outsource it ("people are saying…")

Why not the sandwich? Praise → criticism → praise trains people to brace every time you compliment them, and the real message gets lost between two slices of filler. Be kind and direct: that's the Radical Candor quadrant you'll learn in the next step.

Roleplay: deliver the feedback to "Alex"

🎮 Job Simulation · Interactive & Graded

Have the real conversation — with a simulated direct report

You manage Alex, a talented engineer whose code is strong but who has been dismissive of teammates in reviews — leaving curt comments and overriding decisions without discussion. Two people have quietly told you they're hesitant to push back on Alex. In this roleplay you'll open the conversation, deliver the feedback using SBI, and respond live as Alex reacts — Alex may get defensive, go quiet, or push back. You'll be scored on three dimensions, and the path completes only when you pass.

💚 Empathy
Did you acknowledge Alex's perspective and stay regulated under pushback?
🎯 Clarity
Was the behavior specific and the impact concrete — no vague verdicts?
🤝 Outcome
Did you land a concrete, mutually-agreed next step?
⏱ Est. 12–18 min 🔁 Unlimited retries 🎙 Voice or chat 📊 Pass threshold: 70 / 100
Completion is driven by the simulation result. Unlike a video or PDF, this step has no "Mark complete" button — the platform marks it done (and verifies Difficult Conversations) only when your score ≥ 70. Your transcript and per-dimension scores sync to your manager's dashboard.
